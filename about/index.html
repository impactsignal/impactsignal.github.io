About Impact Signals

Social impact at the scale of AI.

Impact Signals delivers daily AI-powered intelligence for people working at the intersection of technology and social impact. We track how artificial intelligence is being deployed ‚Äî and how it should be deployed ‚Äî for disaster relief, humanitarian operations, climate resilience, public health, and social good worldwide.

Our Mission

The humanitarian sector faces unprecedented challenges: more active conflicts than any point since World War II, collapsing aid budgets, and 117+ million forcibly displaced people. At the same time, AI capabilities are advancing at extraordinary speed ‚Äî from flood forecasting that saves lives in Africa to emergency response toolkits that compress weeks of work into minutes.

Impact Signals exists to bridge this gap. We scan hundreds of sources daily to surface the most important developments in AI for social impact, so practitioners, policymakers, researchers, and funders can make informed decisions quickly.

What We Cover

  • AI for Disaster Response: Early warning systems, search-and-rescue robotics, logistics optimization, damage assessment
  • Humanitarian Technology: Supply chain AI, cash transfer optimization, displacement tracking, health systems
  • Climate & Resilience: Flood forecasting, wildfire detection, heatwave prediction, climate modeling
  • Policy & Governance: UN AI initiatives, AI safety reports, regulatory developments, ethical frameworks
  • AI for Health: Medical imaging, disease surveillance, health worker training, telemedicine
  • Events & Opportunities: Conferences, grants, accelerators, and calls for proposals

Our Approach

Each daily briefing includes:

  • 8 curated stories with full analysis and source attribution
  • Framework tags categorizing each story's impact dimension (Resilience, Response, Relief, Recovery, Enablement, Co-Creation)
  • Upcoming events relevant to the AI-for-impact community
  • Active disaster monitoring from GDACS and OCHA

Who It's For

Impact Signals is designed for:

  • Humanitarian practitioners and NGO workers
  • Social impact professionals and funders
  • AI researchers working on applications for good
  • Policymakers in disaster risk reduction and AI governance
  • Emergency management professionals
  • Students and academics in humanitarian technology
  • Anyone interested in how AI can serve humanity's most vulnerable
