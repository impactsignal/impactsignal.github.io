1. WFP & India Showcase AI-Powered Food Distribution at Scale

The World Food Programme and India's Department of Food & Public Distribution jointly unveiled AI-driven innovations transforming India's Public Distribution System at the India AI Impact Summit 2026. Three operational tools: Grain ATMs delivering precise food entitlements via fully digital systems, Smart Warehouse Solutions using sensor-based AI to prevent food losses, and Route Optimization Functions cutting costs and carbon emissions. WFP needs $13B to assist 110M people in 2026 but faces funding shortfalls forcing ration reductions. WFP's Magan Naidoo: AI is "an accelerator, not a human replacement."

3. NASA Deploys Satellite AI for Cyclone Gezani Damage Assessment

NASA's Earth Observatory used MODIS, Landsat 8 OLI, and IMERG satellite data to map devastation from Cyclone Gezani in Madagascar. Category 3 cyclone with 200 km/h winds killed at least 63 people, injured 804, displaced 16,000+, and destroyed 25,000 homes. Rain rates hit 4 cm/hour over Toamasina. This was Madagascar's second cyclone in 11 days. Copernicus EMSR activated rapid mapping; SADC deployed a geospatial team alongside MapAction and WFP.
