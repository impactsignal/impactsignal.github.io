1. India AI Impact Summit Opens ‚Äî 250,000 Expected at First Global South AI Summit

PM Modi inaugurated the five-day India AI Impact Summit at Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi ‚Äî the largest AI gathering ever hosted in the developing world. With 20 national leaders, 45 ministerial delegations, and CEOs from Google, NVIDIA, Microsoft, and OpenAI, the summit features seven thematic "Chakras" including Safe & Trusted AI, AI for Health, and Inclusion for Social Empowerment. 2. UNDRR: "Glorious AI Apps Will Sit as Dashboards" Without Legal Framework

UNDRR's Sujit Mohanty told the India AI Summit that AI disaster apps are useless without enabling legal frameworks. District magistrates ‚Äî the officials who decide when and whom to evacuate ‚Äî cannot rely on AI predictions without legal accountability. Global disaster losses: ~$200 billion annually. He called for India's "frugal AI" approach to be exported to less-resourced nations, building regulatory scaffolding alongside technology.

Framework tags: Response, Enablement  |  Sources: Business Standard

3. AI Model: Climate Change Could Push 1.1 Billion Into Hunger by 2100

Published in Nature Scientific Reports, an AI model calibrated on FEWS NET data predicts climate change alone could drive 1.1 billion people ‚Äî including 600+ million children ‚Äî into severe food crises. Africa: 170 million face starvation in 2099 alone. The model uses only temperature and precipitation inputs, making it deployable in data-scarce humanitarian regions where traditional models can't reach.

Framework tags: Resilience, Compounding Innovation  |  Sources: Phys.org, Nature Scientific Reports

4. India Launches SAHI & BODH ‚Äî National AI Healthcare Frameworks

Health Minister Nadda announced SAHI (Strategy for AI in Healthcare for India) and BODH (Benchmarking Open Data Platform for Health AI) at the summit. BODH creates open benchmarking datasets for transparent comparison of health AI models ‚Äî a critical gap blocking adoption in low-resource settings. Directly replicable for disaster health response.

Framework tags: Recovery, Co-Creation  |  Sources: The Hindu, Moneycontrol

5. Nonprofit AI Report: 92% Adopt AI, Only 7% See Real Impact

A study of 346 nonprofits reveals a stark "adoption-impact gap." 81% use AI individually without shared workflows. 47% have no AI governance policy. Organizations seeing real impact embed AI into cross-functional workflows ‚Äî not individual heroics. For humanitarian organizations: adoption without workflow redesign is a trap.

Framework tags: Resilience, Enablement  |  Sources: PRNewswire

6. Kerala Outlines AI Roadmap for Disaster Preparedness

At Kerala's "Vision 2031" conference: AI-driven heat stress predictions at granular administrative levels, mobile-first emergency alerts building on 2018 flood lessons, and AI for human-animal conflict management. The mobile-first approach is directly transferable to other climate-vulnerable regions.

Framework tags: Resilience, Enablement  |  Sources: The Hindu

7. China Classifies AI Security Incidents as National Disaster Category

First nation to institutionalize AI containment within civil defense ‚Äî "loss of human control" and "unforeseen incidents" now classified alongside earthquakes in China's National Emergency Response Plan. Sets a precedent for integrating AI risk into existing disaster management infrastructure globally.

Framework tags: Response, Enablement  |  Sources: ASPI Strategist

8. UN Women + Bioniks: AI Prosthetics for Rural Women in Pakistan

Continued deployment of AI-powered, 3D-modeled prosthetic limbs for women in Sindh province who lost limbs to fodder cutter accidents. Customized at dramatically lower costs than imports. "Homegrown AI" from the Global South with direct conflict-zone applicability.

Framework tags: Recovery, Co-Creation  |  Sources: UN.org, Channel Africa
